Thank you for visiting my website. My name is Ransmond Berchie, and I am a third-year Ph.D. Biostatistics student at the Population Health Science Department of University of Utah. I am currently working under Dr. Daniel Scharftein on using EHR data of patients at-risk of sepsis to learn an optimal antibiotic initiation rule , and under Dr. Yue Zhang on using clustering in semi-markov multi-state models to explore the impact of multi-drug resistant organisms (MDRO) co-infection on the probability of progression to ICU care among hospitalized COVID-19 patients. I am broadly interested in causal inference, survival analysis, longitudinal data analysis, and pharmacoepidemiology.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology-Kumasi, Ghana (KNUST). Serving as a Teaching Assistant after my undergraduate study exposed me to numerous research experience which stirred up my interest in how I wanted to use statistics to solve real world problem. In view of that, I participated and presented my undergrdauted project at the Conference on Scientific Computing and Industrial Modeling. This participation led to my first contribution to a publication with my supervisor on Modelling the force of infection for Hepatitis B among heterogeneous groups at the Komfo-Anokye Teaching Hospital.

After serving as a Teaching Assistant for one year at KNUST, I moved to Northern Arizona University (NAU) for my Master's degree in Statistics. My graduate journey at NAU was coupled with teaching responsibilties which gave me the opportunity to impart knowledge and meet people with diverse background. I was awarded Carlson-Diekelman Rushall-Schroeder Memorial Endowed Scholarship for my dedication to teaching and excellence in class as a graduate student. As part of my graduate journey, I used machine learning techniques to predict heart disease, and also employed Bayesian approach to examine factors that strongly influence the presence of heart disease in patients. As a requirement for my master's degree, I also served as a consultant in the Statistical Consulting Lab of the Mathematics and Statistics Department where I provided statistical advice and research assistance to clients in the university community in support of their academic research.

In my free time, I enjoy watching European football and watching drama and thriller series.
